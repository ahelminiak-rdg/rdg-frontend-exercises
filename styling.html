Styling Tips & Tricks

No matter what design you're looking to interpret into code, there are multiple ways to execute it with code styling. However, some ways are more responsive and flexible than others, and often result in cleaner code. The following exercises introduce different styling prompts and layouts, along with best practice ways to accomplish them.

Exercise 1: Flexbox

Flexbox is valuable when you need things to have even spacing and dynamic widths or heights, meaning you need something 'flexible.' It especially comes in handy for things like rows of cards, which is often used these days in web design. Look at the six content pieces below and responsively style them card style, with three per row and equal height on desktop, and one per row with varied height on mobile. You may determine additional breakpoints as you see fit.
